What is IT infrastructure?

Your virtual IT infrastructure is the assets that make up your IT ecosystem. This includes the software, hardware, and additional services necessary for the successful implementation and ongoing management of your IT processes and strategies.

When implemented correctly, an IT infrastructure successfully supports your daily business on-goings, both externally and on a frontline basis.

In a range of industries, a business’s IT infrastructure is often composed of core IT systems, including:

  • Your network is the communicative channels that enable processes, including internet, security, and information storage.

  • IT software is any digital program used to interact and contort data or information. In businesses, this includes customer relationship management software, applications designed for managing productivity, digital bookkeeping programs, and more.

  • Hardware is IT machinery, with each asset carrying a specific purpose in the functionality of your IT ecosystem. Examples of hardware include your computers, hubs, data centres, and routers.

What is IT monitoring?

IT monitoring allows you a unique point of view into the health and functionality of your IT system. This includes the usage of your IT network, its behaviour, and the overall impact of various changes and enhancements on an ongoing basis.

What is IT alerting?

IT alerting refers to the process of notifying systems or individuals in a business as to the on goings of your IT network. This helps keep businesses informed as to how their IT infrastructure is functioning, and demonstrates where potential problems or impactful errors may lay.
