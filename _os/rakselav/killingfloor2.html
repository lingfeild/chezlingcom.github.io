Eschewing a competitive focus in favour of encouraging co-operative gameplay, this horror-themed shooter developed by Tripwire Interactive pits groups of up to six players against an aggressive horde of the undead that become ever-more perilous with each wave that the players eliminate.

Rewarding teams for the kills that they earn at the end of each round with experience and in-game currency that can be spent on more powerful, guns, upgrades, and abilities, Killing Floor 2 provides teams that work together and communicate a significant advantage. The class-based gameplay makes differing play styles equally viable, ensuring that every member of the team can find a role that suits them best.

Thanks to its in-depth progression system, satisfying gameplay, and destructible environments, Killing Floor 2 continues to maintain a healthy multiplayer scene, with dedicated servers becoming the preferred connection choice amongst gamers looking to avoid the slower speeds, connectivity issues, and input lag inherent with peer-to-peer connections.
